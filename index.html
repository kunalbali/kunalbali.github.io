About

I am a PhD student in Environmental Chemistry at the University of Alaska Fairbanks, Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry and Geophysical Institute, under the guidance of Dr. Jingqiu Mao. My research focuses on understanding the optical properties and composition of atmospheric aerosols, particularly black carbon (BC) and brown carbon (BrC), with an emphasis on summer wildfire pollution and winter pollution in high-latitude environments.

Nationality: Indian | DOB: August 29, 1987 | Location: Fairbanks, Alaska, USA

Research Focus

My research investigates the composition and optical properties of atmospheric aerosols in extreme environments-spanning from the Arctic to India‚Äîto understand how wildfire emissions shape climate and air quality across contrasting high-latitude and tropical regions.

Optical properties, absorption, wavelength dependence, and climate forcing

Wildfire Emissions
